About Intermodulation in TETRA Systems

Intermodulation (IM) products occur when two or more frequencies mix within a non-linear device such as a transmitter amplifier. These unwanted signals can cause interference to other receivers.

  • 3rd Order (IM3): Typically the strongest and most problematic type of intermodulation interference. These products follow the formula 2f‚ÇÅ - f‚ÇÇ or 2f‚ÇÇ - f‚ÇÅ.

When planning a TETRA system, it's important to consider these potential intermodulation products to ensure proper frequency spacing and avoid interference issues.
